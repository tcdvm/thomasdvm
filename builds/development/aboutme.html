About Me

About Me

I'm originally from San Francisco and went to UCLA for my undergraduate degree. I started off as Microbiology/Genetics major and then switched to computer science after my first year. After graduating I got a job with the Boeing Co working on databases involved cost accounting. It is just as exciting as it sounds. About 6 months into the job, sitting in my cubicle, bored to tears, I stared at my monitor and thought to myself "I have to get out of here."

I thought long and hard about what I could do that would be the antithesis of where I was (in a cubicle working on cost-accounting databases) and thought of doing medicine. I liked biology, wanted to do something meaningful with a positive impact on people (vs making sure every fraction of a cent was accounted for to the government client for Boeing). I didn't want to work on people and so my fate was decided then and there. Over the next 1.5 years I gradually accumulated the pre-reqs I needed to apply to vet school and the rest, as they say, is history.

How I got into veterinary ophthalmology is a story for another time but I have no regrets and am very happy and fortunate to be where I am today.

Thank you for reading.
